# Cat Culture Voice Bible - 2026-07-13

Purpose: give Counter Cat and future animal puzzle games a durable cat-culture language system: funny, current, recognizably cat-person, and not just a pile of random puns.

## Research takeaways

1. Cats are not just cute online. They are a long-running internet language.
   - Cat meme history predates modern social platforms, but internet cat culture became especially visible through lolcats, Keyboard Cat, Grumpy Cat, Nyan Cat, Lil Bub, Henri, Maru, and later short-form video formats.
   - The best cat humor treats cats as interpretable little characters: scientist, criminal, landlord, roommate, tiny tyrant, goblin-mode roommate, bored genius, soft baby, dramatic aristocrat.
   - Source: Hyperrhiz / University of Illinois cat meme history; Walker Art Center Internet Cat Video Festival archive.

2. Cat people are a real public culture, not a niche embarrassment.
   - CatCon says it has welcomed over 100,000 attendees since 2015.
   - CatCon and modern cat-lady writing frame cat fandom as proud, communal, stylish, adoption-positive, and often self-aware.
   - Use affectionate identity language: cat parent, staff, servant, roommate, tiny landlord, unpaid intern to a cat.
   - Avoid sneering at cat people. The player should feel seen, not mocked.

3. The knocking-things-off premise is behaviorally grounded.
   - PetMD and PetPlace summarize common reasons: instinct/prey drive, curiosity, play, boredom, sensory feedback, and learned attention/reaction reinforcement.
   - This is exactly the game loop: paw tap -> motion/noise -> human reaction -> reward.
   - The joke should be: the cat is not evil; the cat is conducting research and training the human.

4. Cat videos work because they are mood repair.
   - Jessica Myrick's 2015 Computers in Human Behavior study and Indiana University coverage reported that cat video watching was associated with higher positive emotion/energy and lower negative emotion after viewing.
   - Design implication: keep Counter Cat low-stress. No timers, no guilt, no harsh fail states. It should feel like a tiny mood reset.

5. Real cat body language gives us better jokes than generic puns.
   - The slow blink has scientific support as positive cat-human communication; a 2020 Scientific Reports paper found human slow-blink interactions made cats more receptive/approach-oriented.
   - Boxes are grounded in comfort/security/control; PopSci and Discover summarize research and expert explanations around enclosed spaces lowering stress and giving ambush/control advantages.
   - Use real behaviors: slow blink, loaf, zoomies, kneading, chirping, chattering, bap/bap-bap, head-butt/bunt, biscuit-making, tail twitch, airplane ears, side-eye, midnight hallway sprint, sitting in boxes, ignoring expensive toys.

## Core voice

Counter Cat voice should be:

- Dry, deadpan, short.
- Affectionate toward the cat and the player.
- Written from a world where cat logic is normal and human logic is secondary.
- Confident that destruction is science.
- More "crime report" than "LOL random".
- More behavior-specific than pun-stuffed.

Good example tone:

- "Gravity was tested. Results were conclusive."
- "The yarn was protected at all costs."
- "Human reaction: reinforcing."
- "Counter cleared. Cat unavailable for comment."
- "The mug has entered floor custody."

Weak tone:

- "That was purr-fectly claw-some!" repeated everywhere.
- "Meow meow meow!" with no joke.
- Any copy that implies the player is cruel or the cat is malicious.

## Humor engines to use

### 1. Cat as scientist

Best for Counter Cat because the mechanic is literally testing physics.

- Gravity: confirmed.
- Hypothesis: mug falls. Result: mug falls.
- Further research required.
- Peer reviewed by one cat.
- Methodology: paw.
- The control group was also pushed.
- Experiment replicated.
- Results were audible.
- Human response observed.
- Conclusion: do it again tomorrow.

### 2. Cat as criminal / heist artist

Best for win cards, par/stars, share text, achievements.

- A perfect crime.
- Clean getaway.
- No witnesses, only paw prints.
- Evidence relocated.
- Floor custody.
- Case closed.
- Suspect is extremely soft.
- The defendant slow-blinked.
- Alibi: asleep the whole time.
- Crime scene: adorable.

### 3. Cat as tiny boss / landlord

Best for UI, onboarding, progression, daily job.

- Report for cat duty.
- Today's cat job.
- Staff performance review.
- Counter inspection.
- Human has been notified.
- Rent paid in gravity.
- The cat has standards.
- This counter is under new management.

### 4. Cat as bored genius

Best for loading, hints, fail/refusal copy.

- Indoor enrichment achieved.
- The puzzle was in the human all along.
- Boredom found a solution.
- Static objects are suspicious.
- If it moves, inspect it. If it does not move, make it move.
- The cat has created a task.

### 5. Cat as soft baby with no accountability

Best as occasional contrast after destruction.

- Still baby.
- Too cute to prosecute.
- Tiny paws, large consequences.
- Innocent by fluff.
- The face says no regrets.
- Please respect the loaf.

## Future games using cat language

### Pup & Purr Bento

Cat language should contrast with dog language.

Cats:
- selective
- suspicious
- tiny food critic
- fish/yarn/milk/sunbeam
- "I requested this, but not like that."

Dogs:
- delighted
- fetch/treat/park/wag
- "10/10 snack, no notes."

Good copy:
- Cat cafe shift
- Dog park lunch rush
- Shared treat treaty
- Fish for the critic, bones for the optimist
- The cat inspected the bento and blinked slowly

### Paws & Yarn Tangle

Use cat/yarn craft-room culture.

- The yarn did nothing wrong.
- The cat improved the craft project.
- Untangle before the dog thinks it is fetch.
- Thread custody restored.
- Knots removed. Dignity pending.

### Pet Parade Sort

Cats should resist formation; dogs should be eager.

- Cats do not line up. They permit arrangements.
- Dogs are already ready.
- The rabbit has notes.
- Park photo in progress.
- Treat-based diplomacy.

### Mosaic Meadow

Use cats lightly.

- garden patrol
- bee inspection
- sunbeam tile
- cat cameo on win, not every tile
- "The cat approved the path by sitting on it."

## What to avoid

- Too many puns in a row. Cat people like puns, but they also like specificity.
- Mocking cat ladies or treating cat ownership as lonely/weird. Modern cat culture has reclaimed this identity; be affectionate and proud.
- Copying protected cat characters or phrases too closely: Grumpy Cat, Nyan Cat, Keyboard Cat branding/likeness, specific celebrity cats, Cats Knocking Things Off Ledges.
- Making cats seem cruel. Better framing: curious, bored, scientific, attention-trained, dramatic, soft, unbothered.
- Pun-only names that hide the mechanic. The store screenshot has to say what the game does.

## Implementation recommendations

1. Add copy pools by situation rather than one global joke pool:
   - crashToasts
   - blockedYarnToasts
   - winTitles
   - receiptFooters
   - hintToasts
   - dailyMissions
   - achievementNames

2. Keep sentence length short in toasts.
   - Best: 3 to 8 words.
   - Longer jokes belong in receipts, galleries, or level names.

3. Make the cat physically react to copy beats:
   - slow blink on win
   - tail twitch on blocked move
   - paw hover before crash
   - side-eye during Daily Job
   - red-dot interruption as a short optional gag

4. Make share cards read like artifacts:
   - Crime report
   - Lab result
   - Staff memo
   - Floor inventory

5. For phone UI, prefer icons plus short labels:
   - Daily Job
   - Gallery
   - Report
   - Replay
   - Fast
